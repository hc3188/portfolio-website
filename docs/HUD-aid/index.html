How the Department of Housing and Urban Development is failing the country’s poorest counties

By Harrison Connery

These visualizations were inspired by a story that ran in the New York Times about economic revitalization in West Virginia. The story mentioned that federal housing aid from the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development is tied to a county’s local median income. The result is that people in the nation’s poorest counties are automatically disqualified from federal aid. I decided to use HUD data to map the counties and investigate the discrepancy between what amounts to a living wage in those areas and the limit for aid.

In a majority of these states more than 50% of counties do not qualify for federal aid.

By breaking the median income down to the hourly equivalent, it becomes clear that aid limits are well below the living wage threshold.
